# Global Threat Landscape (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary

The global cybersecurity threat landscape in 2024-2026 is characterized by unprecedented attack volumes, sophisticated nation-state operations, and the weaponization of artificial intelligence. Organizations worldwide face a 72% increase in cyber threats year-over-year, with ransomware incidents up 46% and AI-powered attacks becoming mainstream.

## Key Statistics Overview

| Metric | 2024-2025 Value | Change |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Weekly Cyberattacks per Organization | 1,925 | +47% from 2024 |
| Global Ransomware Victims | 7,515+ | +53-63% YoY |
| Vulnerabilities Disclosed | +31% YoY | +20% active exploitation |
| Average US Data Breach Cost | $10 million | 2x global average |

## 1. Major Cyber Incidents (2024-2026)

### Tier 1: Critical Infrastructure and Government

#### Salt Typhoon Telecom Intrusion (2024)
- **Scope**: Compromised at least 9 major US telecommunications providers
- **Impact**: Exfiltrated data including law enforcement wiretapping requests; accessed phones of presidential candidates
- **Attribution**: China (PRC)-linked APT group
- **Significance**: Most unprecedented telecom intrusion in US history

#### Critical Infrastructure Pre-Positioning (2024-2025)
- PRC-backed cyber actors maintained access for months within networks of public power utilities (e.g., Littleton, Massachusetts)
- Purpose: Pre-positioning for potential disruptive operations during geopolitical crisis
- Expanded beyond energy to telecommunications, water, and transportation sectors

#### Italian National Energy Grid Attack (2024)
- Sophisticated cyberattack disrupted Italy's national energy grid
- Raised significant concerns about European critical infrastructure security

### Tier 2: Healthcare and Financial Services

#### Change Healthcare Ransomware Attack (Early 2024)
- **Target**: Major provider of revenue cycle management and clinical information exchange
- **Impact**: Widespread disruption to healthcare payment processing across the United States
- **Cascading Effects**: Delayed patient care, billing disruptions affecting thousands of healthcare providers

#### Transport for London (TfL) Attack (September 2024)
- **Cost**: 30 million (approximately $38M USD)
- **Impact**: Disrupted services affecting millions of commuters; particularly impacted differently-abled passengers relying on Dial-A-Ride service
- **Duration**: Extended recovery period affecting multiple systems

### Tier 3: Technology and Data Breaches

#### Ivanti Zero-Day Exploits (January 2024)
- **Vulnerabilities**: Two zero-days in Connect Secure and Policy Secure gateways
- **Impact**: Over 1,700 ICS VPN appliances compromised with GIFTEDVISITOR web shell by mid-January
- **Attackers**: Multiple threat actors engaged in mass exploitation

#### T-Mobile Data Breach (2024)
- Another major data breach exposing customer information including addresses and payment details
- Part of ongoing pattern of telecom sector targeting

#### India Aadhaar/Passport Data Leak
- Millions of citizens' Aadhaar and passport details leaked
- One of the largest identity data exposures in history

## 2. Emerging Threat Vectors

### AI-Powered Attacks

#### Phishing Evolution
| Metric | 2025 Value |
|--------|------------|
| AI-generated phishing emails | 82.6% of all phishing |
| Phishing increase (YoY) | +1,265% |
| Polymorphic attacks using LLMs | 90%+ |

- AI-generated phishing has become the top enterprise email threat, surpassing ransomware and traditional social engineering
- Large Language Models (LLMs) enable creation of contextually accurate, grammatically perfect phishing at scale

#### Deepfake Fraud
- **Financial Impact**: Individual incidents reaching $25.6M
- **Technical Capability**: Synthetic voices and video now indistinguishable from real content in real-time
- **Attack Vector**: Multi-modal AI combines facial data, voice samples, and text for comprehensive identity impersonation
- **Bypass Capability**: Can defeat voice-activated banking, facial recognition, and online verification systems

#### Autonomous AI Attacks
- November 2025: First documented large-scale cyberattack executed without substantial human intervention
- AI tools leveraged for fully automated sophisticated attacks targeting technology companies, financial institutions, manufacturing, and government agencies

### Social Engineering at Scale

- **Organizations Experiencing AI-Powered Attacks**: 87% in 2025
- **AI Kits Sold on Dark Web (Q1 2025)**: 8,500+
- **AI-Driven Transactions on Dark Web**: 32% of all transactions

### Supply Chain Attacks

#### XZ Backdoor (Early 2024)
- Malicious code injected into popular Linux-based IoT software
- Risked enabling remote code execution across thousands of deployments
- Highlighted vulnerability of open-source software supply chains

#### Third-Party Vendor Compromise
- Hardware backdoors and tampered firmware during manufacturing
- Persistent, hard-to-detect threats through legitimate supply chain channels

- Multi-extortion schemes (encryption + data theft) remain prominent

## 4. Nation-State Cyber Activities

### Overview

By 2025, Microsoft tracks over 600 distinct nation-state hacker groups worldwide. State-aligned hacking groups have intensified espionage, sabotage, and cybercrime campaigns across multiple regions.

### China (PRC)

**Threat Level**: Most active and persistent cyber threat to US government and critical sectors

#### Key Groups and Campaigns

| Group | Focus Areas | 2024-2025 Activity |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Salt Typhoon | Telecommunications | Compromised 9+ major US telecoms |
| Mustang Panda | Diplomacy, Technology | Ongoing espionage campaigns |
| APT41 | Technology, Manufacturing | Continued operations |

#### July 2025 Incident
- Three PRC-associated threat actors compromised 400+ organizations through Microsoft SharePoint
- Targets included: Department of Energy, Department of Homeland Security, Department of Health and Human Services

#### Strategic Intent
- Pre-positioning within critical infrastructure networks (water, energy, telecommunications)
- Establishing persistent access for potential disruption during geopolitical crises
- Intelligence collection on government activities and policy decisions

### Russia

**Focus**: Ukraine conflict support, Western intelligence collection, destructive operations

#### Key Groups

**Sandworm (GRU)**
- Continued destructive attacks inside Ukraine
- Deployed data-wiping malware: ZEROLOT, Sting
- Targets: Government, energy, logistics, grain-sector organizations
- Goal: Weakening Ukraine's wartime economy

**Fancy Bear (Unit 26165 GRU)**
- Carried out campaign targeting Western logistics and technology entities for 2+ years
- Reported by NSA and partner agencies in 2025

### Iran

**Trend**: 133% spike in cyberattacks (May-June 2024 vs March-April) amid US and Israeli airstrikes

#### Key Groups

| Group | Alternative Name | Focus |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| OilRig | APT34 | Regional surveillance, telecommunications, energy, government |
| MuddyWater | - | Internal spearphishing, bypassing perimeter defenses |

**Notable TTP**: MuddyWater compromises internal mailboxes then sends phishing from trusted accounts to coworkers, effectively bypassing traditional defenses.

### North Korea

**Primary Motivation**: Regime financing through cryptocurrency theft

#### Key Groups
- **Lazarus Group**: Cryptocurrency theft, financial sector targeting
- **Kimsuky**: Espionage operations, social engineering

**Characteristics**:
- Social engineering and breach of trust systems
- Targeting VPNs and remote access tools
- Cryptocurrency exchange and DeFi platform attacks

### Geographic Impact (US)

**2025 State/Local Government Incidents:**
- 44+ US states reported cyber incidents affecting state and local government systems
- Communities declared states of emergency following major intrusions (St. Paul, Minnesota; Mission, Texas)

## 7. 2025-2026 Threat Predictions

### CrowdStrike 2025 Global Threat Report Key Findings

- Social engineering, cloud intrusions, and malware-free techniques surged
- Nation-state actors intensified cyber espionage
- AI added to adversary arsenal across all threat categories

### Recorded Future Predictions

- More high-profile breaches of US critical infrastructure by China-linked APTs
- Expansion beyond energy and telecommunications sectors
- Breaches aimed at pre-positioning for disruptive operations

### Trend Micro Warnings

- Ransomware attacks will become faster and more automated
- AI capabilities will power both attack generation and evasion
- Healthcare sector particularly at risk in 2026
