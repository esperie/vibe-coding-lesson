## Executive Summary

Singapore has established itself as a leading cybersecurity hub in the Asia-Pacific region, driven by strong government leadership through the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA), a robust regulatory framework, and significant investments in talent and innovation. The nation faces an evolving threat landscape, particularly from state-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs), while managing the dual challenge of digital transformation and cyber resilience.

### Key Highlights

- **Market Size**: USD 2.65 billion in 2025, projected to reach USD 5.60 billion by 2030 (16.14% CAGR)
- **Workforce Gap**: ~4,000 unfilled cybersecurity positions; 39 cybersecurity roles on the Shortage of Labour list
- **Threat Escalation**: 49% surge in phishing cases in 2024; 4x increase in APT incidents since 2021
- **Regional Leadership**: Host of ASEAN Regional CERT (commenced October 2024); USD 10.1 million commitment over 10 years
- **R&D Investment**: S$62 million for CyberSG R&D Programme; S$16 million awarded in 2025 for innovation projects
