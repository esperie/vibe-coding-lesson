## Top Cyber Threats Facing Singapore

### Threat Statistics Overview (2024)

| Threat Category | 2024 Value | Change YoY | Notes |
|-----------------|------------|------------|-------|
| **Phishing Cases** | 6,100+ | +49% | AI-generated content rising |
| **Ransomware Cases** | 159 | +21% | MNCs and SMEs targeted |
| **Infected Infrastructure** | 117,300 systems | +67% | From 70,200 in 2023 |
| **Scam Losses** | S$1.1 billion | Persistent | Net ~S$930M after recovery |
| **AI-Generated Phishing** | 12% of emails | New metric | Emerging trend |

### Global Context

| Metric | Singapore's Position |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Most Attacked Countries (Q4 2024)** | 7th globally |
| **DDoS Attack Traffic Source** | 3rd largest |
| **APT Incident Growth (2021-2024)** | 4x increase |

### 1. Phishing and Social Engineering

**Scale**: 49% surge to 6,100+ cases in 2024

**Evolution**:
- 12% of phishing emails contain AI-generated content
- Increasingly sophisticated social engineering
- Multi-channel attacks (email, SMS, voice)

**Most Spoofed Industries**:
1. Banking and Financial Services (63% in 2023)
2. Government
3. E-commerce

**Key Characteristics**:
- Convincing impersonation of banks and government agencies
- Use of AI to create more convincing content
- Targeted attacks on high-value individuals

### 2. Ransomware

**Scale**: 159 reported cases in 2024 (+21% YoY)

**Primary Targets**:
| Sector | Attack Share | Target Profile |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **Manufacturing** | 31% | Largest target sector |
| **Professional Services** | High | SMEs in consulting, legal, accounting |
| **Technology** | Significant | Data-rich targets |

**Tactics**:
- Double extortion (data theft + encryption)
- Targeting of MNCs and listed firms
- Disproportionate SME targeting

**Notable Incident (March 2025)**:
- Ransomware attack on Singapore-based IT services provider
- Compromised personal data of 100,000+ individuals
- Disrupted operations across multiple public sector agencies

### 3. Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)

**Scale**: 4x increase in suspected APT incidents (2021-2024)

**Primary Threat Actor**: UNC3886
- State-sponsored (implicitly Chinese)
- Active since late 2021
- First publicly named July 18, 2025

**UNC3886 Targets in Singapore**:
- Critical national infrastructure
- Banking systems
- Energy grids
- Water networks
- Transport hubs

**TTPs**:
- Living off the Land (LoTL) techniques
- Zero-day exploits
- High-value strategic targeting
- Long dwell times

**Other Active Groups**:
- Volt Typhoon (Singtel breach, November 2024)
- Mustang Panda (ASEAN-Australia Summit timing)
- APT41, APT10, Salt Typhoon

### 4. Supply Chain Attacks

**Impact**: 93% of Singapore organizations negatively impacted by supply chain breaches (+23% YoY)

**Notable Incidents**:
| Incident | Impact | Lesson |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Toppan Next Tech (TNT)** | Banks affected (DBS, Bank of China Singapore) | Non-core vendors create risk |
| **CrowdStrike Outage (2024)** | US$5.4B losses to Fortune 500 | Faulty update, not attack |
| **IT Services Provider (Mar 2025)** | 100K+ individuals, public sector agencies | Cascading impact |

### 5. Infected Infrastructure

**Scale**: 117,300 systems compromised in 2024 (+67% YoY)

**Types**:
- Botnets
- Command and control infrastructure
- Compromised edge devices
- Malware-infected systems

**Concern**: Singapore as 3rd largest source of DDoS attack traffic (Q4 2024)

### 6. Scams and Fraud

**Total Losses (2024)**: S$1.1 billion
**Net Losses After Recovery**: ~S$930 million

**Context**: Cybercrime made up 43% of all crime in Singapore (2022)

**Key Vectors**:
- Investment scams
- E-commerce fraud
- Job scams
- Love scams
- Government impersonation

## Short-Term Priority Areas (2025-2026)

### Government Priority Initiatives

| Initiative | Timeline | Focus |
|------------|----------|-------|
| **Cybersecurity Act Amendments** | Effective Oct 31, 2025 | New regulated categories |
| **Digital Infrastructure Act** | Introduction 2025 | Data centres, cloud security |
| **Cyber Essentials/Trust Update** | Mid-2025 | Cloud and AI guidance |
| **Quantum-Safe Guidelines** | 2025 rollout | Post-quantum cryptography |
| **Agentic AI Security Addendum** | Consultation to Dec 2025 | AI agent security |
| **Counter Ransomware Initiative Summit** | Oct 24, 2025 | Singapore hosting |

### Strategic Priorities

#### 1. Expanding Regulatory Coverage

**New Categories Under Cybersecurity Act**:
- Systems of Temporary Cybersecurity Concern (STCC)
- Entities of Special Cybersecurity Interest (ESCI)
- Foundational Digital Infrastructure (FDI)
- Provider-Owned CII (PO CII)

**Focus**: Beyond traditional CII to cloud, data centres, essential digital services

#### 2. APT Defense Enhancement

**Drivers**:
- 4x increase in APT incidents
- UNC3886 targeting critical infrastructure
- Sophisticated TTPs requiring advanced detection

**Actions**:
- Enhanced CII incident reporting (2-hour window for APT-related)
- Exercise Cyber Star (largest edition in 2024)
- CIDeX with AI-driven attack simulations

#### 3. Third-Party Risk Management

**Driver**: 93% breach impact rate from supply chain

**Focus Areas**:
- Vendor security assessments
- SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) adoption
- Continuous monitoring
- Contractual security requirements

#### 4. AI Security

**Two Dimensions**:
1. **Securing AI Systems**: Guidelines launched October 2024
2. **AI-Enabled Threats**: Defensive AI, detecting AI-generated attacks

**Priority**: Agentic AI security (new addendum in consultation)

#### 5. Quantum Readiness

**Timeline**: Early 2030s for quantum threat maturity

**Immediate Risk**: Harvest Now, Decrypt Later (HNDL) attacks

**Actions**:
- Quantum-Safe Handbook (CSA)
- Quantum Readiness Index
- MAS Advisory to financial institutions
- S$295 million additional investment (2024)

## AI Security and Quantum Readiness

### AI Security Framework

#### Guidelines on Securing AI Systems (October 2024)

**Scope**: Best practices across AI lifecycle

| Phase | Key Considerations |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Design** | Threat modeling, secure architecture |
| **Development** | Secure coding, supply chain |
| **Deployment** | Configuration, access control |
| **Operation** | Monitoring, incident response |
| **Disposal** | Data handling, model decommission |

**Identified Threats**:
- Supply chain attacks on AI components
- Adversarial Machine Learning
- Data poisoning
- Model theft
- Prompt injection

**Reference Standards**:
- UK NCSC/US CISA Guidelines
- NIST Risk Management Framework
- MITRE ATLAS
- OWASP ML Security Top 10

#### Agentic AI Security Addendum (2025)

**Public Consultation**: October 22 - December 31, 2025

**Focus**: Securing Agentic AI systems with:
- Independent action capabilities
- Tool and data access
- Context understanding
- Plan formulation

**New Risks**:
- Greater potential impact from autonomous actions
- Tool misuse or manipulation
- Data access scope management

| Application | Trend |
|-------------|-------|
| **Phishing Content Generation** | 12% of phishing emails AI-generated |
| **Research Acceleration** | Faster vulnerability discovery |
| **Code Troubleshooting** | Malware development assistance |
| **Deepfakes** | Fraud, impersonation |
| **Social Engineering** | More convincing attacks |

### AI as Defense Tool

| Application | Adoption |
|-------------|----------|
| **Threat Detection** | 90%+ in Singapore/Thailand |
| **Continuous Monitoring** | 64% identify as best AI use |
| **Incident Response** | Automated triage and analysis |
| **Threat Intelligence** | Pattern recognition, correlation |

### Quantum Security

#### Timeline

| Phase | Expected |
|-------|----------|
| **Current** | Harvest Now, Decrypt Later active |
| **Near-Term (2025-2030)** | Migration planning, standards adoption |
| **Medium-Term (Early 2030s)** | Cryptographically-relevant quantum computers |

#### Singapore's Response

| Initiative | Status | Purpose |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **Quantum-Safe Handbook** | Launched 2025 | Practical guidance |
| **Quantum Readiness Index** | Launched 2025 | Assessment tool |
| **CSA Guidelines** | Rollout from 2025 | Regulatory framework |
| **MAS Advisory** | Feb 2024 | Financial sector guidance |
| **S$295M Investment** | 2024 | Quantum technology R&D |

#### Two Approaches to Quantum Security

| Approach | Description | Status |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)** | Math-based algorithms resistant to quantum | NIST standards 2024 (ML-KEM, ML-DSA) |
| **Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)** | Physics-based key exchange | Infrastructure-dependent |

**PQC Advantage**: Often deployable through software upgrades

#### Industry Solutions

**Singtel Hybrid Quantum-Safe Network**:
- Enterprise data protection
- Scalable transition approach
- Preparation for quantum era

## Recommendations for Government Cybersecurity Unit

### Immediate Priorities (2025)

1. **APT Detection and Response**: Build capabilities to detect UNC3886-style attacks
2. **Incident Reporting**: Ensure 2-hour notification capability for APT incidents
3. **Supply Chain Assessment**: Audit critical third-party relationships
4. **AI Security**: Implement guidelines for AI systems in use
5. **Quantum Planning**: Begin post-quantum migration assessment

### Medium-Term (2025-2026)

1. **Zero Trust Implementation**: Modernize security architecture
2. **OT Security Enhancement**: Apply Masterplan 2024 requirements
3. **Cloud Security**: Prepare for Digital Infrastructure Act
4. **Talent Development**: Address skill gaps through training programs
5. **Regional Coordination**: Leverage ASEAN CERT for threat sharing

### Operational Focus

1. **Threat Intelligence**: Develop APT tracking and attribution capabilities
2. **Exercise Participation**: Engage in Cyber Star, CIDeX
3. **Cross-Sector Coordination**: Build relationships with CII sectors
4. **International Cooperation**: Maintain Five Eyes-aligned partnerships
5. **Public Communication**: Balance transparency with operational security
